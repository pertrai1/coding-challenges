# Technical Interviewing Rubrics Template

Each category should be scored on a scale from **1–5**:

- **1** – Major deficiencies, significant help required
- **2** – Below expectations, frequent guidance needed
- **3** – Meets expectations, solid but unexceptional
- **4** – Above expectations, mostly independent and strong
- **5** – Exceptional, highly efficient, clear mastery

---

## Problem Solving

**Score (1–5):**

- How much difficulty did they have finding an algorithm and optimizing it, relative to the difficulty of the problem?
- How much help did they need, relative to other candidates?
- Did they discuss tradeoffs in terms of time and space complexity?
- Did they use appropriate data structures and algorithms to solve the problem?

---

## Coding

**Score (1–5):**

- Could they translate their ideas into correct code?
- Did they overcomplicate the logic?
- Did they demonstrate good coding hygiene (clear variable names, reusable helper functions instead of copy/paste, consistent and reasonable style, etc.)?
- Is the code well organized and idiomatic to the language used?
- Did they demonstrate internalized software engineering principles?

---

## Verification

**Score (1–5):**

- Did they ask good clarifying questions (that weren’t already answered in the description or examples)?
- Did they proactively consider edge cases?
- Did they test their code or provide a sound argument for its correctness?
- Were they able to spot and fix any bugs?

---

## Communication

**Score (1–5):**

- Were they able to clearly communicate their thoughts, even when the conversation became technical?
- Were they able to answer technical questions?
- Were they open to feedback?
- If they were confused, were they able to articulate why?

---

## Overall Recommendation

**Classification:** _Recommend / Lean Recommend / Neutral / Lean No / Do Not Recommend_
